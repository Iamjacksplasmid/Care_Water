A Day in the Life of a Young Girl’s Quest for Water.

See what Precious had to do every day before you helped change her life and her village.

Timothy Buckley/CARE

Journey to the River

6:00AM

I arise early so I can get water from the river before school. I am thirsty, so I drink some of the water I collected yesterday. It is not very clear, but I must drink it anyway.

Timothy Buckley/CARE

 

6:15AM

I get my bucket and start my 2-mile walk to the river.

6:30AM

I can smell a snake in the nearby tree, so I walk around it.

laura-barry-unsplash

6:40AM

This part of the river is where hippos live. I keep a safe distance and an eye on them because they are territorial and can attack quickly.

lisette-verwoerd-unsplash

Alana Holmberg/CARE Australia

On the Bank of the River

7:00AM

I arrive at the place on the river where I can access the water. I must wait my turn, since someone from every family in my village comes here for water. When it is my turn, a man with cattle moves his herd out of the way. I can see cattle feces floating nearby.

John Hewat/CARE

 

7:10AM

I walk into the murky water and look hard with each step to make certain there are no crocodiles lurking beneath before I dip my bucket into the water.

daniel-joshua-unsplash

 

7:11AM

I quickly fill my bucket and get out of the river, looking behind me for crocodiles. Once on the bank, I lift my 5-gallon, 40-pound bucket to my head and start walking back home.

Alana Holmberg/CARE Australia

Heading Home

7:50AM

My tummy is sore and I can tell diarrhea is coming because of the water I drank when I woke up.

Alana Holmberg/CARE Australia

 

8:00AM

I arrive at our house and place the bucket of water inside the door so that everyone in my household can use it. I then go back to my room to lie down because I feel ill.

Alana Holmberg/CARE Australia

 

9:00AM

School started a half hour ago, but I am still not feeling well enough to go.

9:45AM

I’m feeling somewhat better, so I get up, gather my books and walk to school.
I go to a math class that has almost ended.

Timothy Buckley/CARE

 

3:30PM

School is out, so I talk to some of my friends for a while. But I can’t stay long because I must go to the river for water again. I don’t want to go in the dark because it is much more dangerous.

Alana Holmberg/CARE Australia

One Year Later

10:00AM

School was canceled today and nearly everyone in the whole village is standing, talking about the new well that will be dug and pointing excitedly at the truck with the drilling machine. We had never seen one before.

Timothy Buckley/CARE

 

1:00PM

The pipes that they had been putting into the ground all of a sudden began spraying lots of water into the air. It was coming down like rain. Everyone in the village began shouting, singing and dancing!

Timothy Buckley/CARE

 

Today

This is our well. It has clean water without germs. Everyone uses it and no one gets sick. We use it for our crops, too.

I no longer need to travel to the river to get water, and no crocodiles, hippos or snakes come here.

I am doing well in school and I no longer miss any days because of sickness from contaminated water. When I finish school, I want to be a geologist.

Thank you, CARE!

Timothy Buckley/CARE
